Sri Lankan Fish Curry
Serves: 4Prep: 15 minCook: 30 min
Ingredients
  • 600g firm white fish, cubed
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp Sri Lankan curry powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 green chillies, sliced
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • Salt to taste
Method
  1. Marinate fish in turmeric, chilli powder, half the curry powder and salt for 15 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a clay pot. Splutter mustard seeds. Add curry leaves, onion and garlic — fry until golden.
  3. Add remaining curry powder and cook 2 minutes. Add tamarind paste and green chillies.
  4. Pour in coconut milk, bring to a gentle simmer.
  5. Add fish and cook 10–12 minutes on low until fish is cooked. Do not stir vigorously — fish will break.
  6. Adjust salt and tamarind. Serve with steamed rice and coconut sambol.
Coconut Sambol
Serves: 6Prep: 10 minCook: 0 min
Ingredients
  • 2 cups desiccated coconut
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp red chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp Maldive fish (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste
Method
  1. If using desiccated coconut, moisten with a splash of water and mix well.
  2. Crush Maldive fish (if using) in a mortar and pestle to a coarse powder.
  3. Combine coconut, onion, chilli flakes and Maldive fish in a bowl.
  4. Add lime juice and salt. Mix well, rubbing the ingredients together with your hands.
  5. Taste and adjust chilli, lime and salt. Should be tangy, spicy and fragrant.
  6. Serve alongside rice & curry, hoppers or string hoppers.
