Kung Pao Chicken
Serves: 3–4Prep: 15 minCook: 15 min
Ingredients
  • 500g chicken thigh, diced
  • 80g roasted peanuts
  • 8–10 dried red chillies
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 spring onions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cornflour
Method
  1. Marinate chicken in soy sauce, Shaoxing wine and cornflour for 15 minutes.
  2. Heat wok over high heat. Add oil and fry dried chillies and Sichuan peppercorns until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  4. Add chicken, spreading out evenly. Let it sear for 1 minute then stir-fry until cooked through.
  5. Mix soy sauce, sugar, a dash of vinegar and cornflour to make sauce. Pour into wok.
  6. Toss through peanuts and spring onions. Finish with sesame oil. Serve immediately with steamed rice.
Pan-Fried Pork Dumplings
Makes: 24 pcsPrep: 45 minCook: 15 min
Ingredients
  • 250g ground pork
  • 100g napa cabbage, salted & squeezed
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 spring onions, finely chopped
  • 24 round dumpling wrappers
  • Dipping: soy sauce, rice vinegar, chilli oil
Method
  1. Mix pork, cabbage, soy sauce, sesame oil, wine, ginger and spring onions. Stir in one direction until sticky.
  2. Place 1 tsp filling in centre of wrapper. Fold and pleat edges to seal.
  3. Heat oil in a flat pan over medium-high heat. Add dumplings flat-side down.
  4. Fry until bases are golden, about 3 minutes. Add 80ml water, cover immediately.
  5. Steam until water evaporates and bases are crispy again, about 6 minutes.
  6. Serve with soy sauce, rice vinegar and chilli oil for dipping.
